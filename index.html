Current status of key nutrition indicators

The prevalence and distribution of malnutrition are affected by where one lives-rural or urban, upland or lowland dwelling; socio economic status or household wealth quintile; access to food and social services. Maternal education, parity and age at birth are associated with malnutrition among children in Lao PDR.

Women Undernutrtion

Maternal nutrition is a key determinant of child nutrition and well being. Poor nutrition before and during pregnancy impacts on birth outcomes and contributes to low birth weight. Prevalence of anaemia among women of reproductive age remains a public health issue in Lao PDR with almost all provinces above the recommended threshold of 20%. 13% of women are overweight with 3.5% suffering from obesity.

Immediate determinants of undernutrition

Poor maternal, infant and young child feeding practices as well as water, sanitation and hygiene related factors are the main drivers of malnutrition in Lao PDR. Maternal income and educational status are closely associated with children's nutritional status.
